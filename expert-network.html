Specialist review network

Help improve industry consistency and structural outcomes.

First Line Structural is building a pool of engineers with deep experience in specific systems, materials, regions, and construction pitfalls. The goal is simple: help project teams catch the right issues earlier.

The role

Provide targeted expertise without taking over the project.

Expert contributors provide scoped preliminary input based on the needs of a project. You may be asked to identify common mistakes, review a detail type, comment on constructability risks, or help frame the questions a city, architect, owner, or primary reviewer should ask.

You are not acting as the Engineer of Record for the project through this network, and you are not expected to stamp drawings. Your role is focused technical judgment: where projects like this commonly go wrong and what deserves closer attention.

Useful specialties

  • Mass timber and hybrid structural systems
  • Steel connections and erection-sensitive details
  • Seismic detailing and capacity design
  • High-wind design and load transfer
  • Masonry, concrete, wood, and cold-formed steel
  • Existing building evaluation and retrofit
  • Industrial, institutional, and unusual building types

What you may contribute

  • Common plan review misses
  • Construction pitfalls you see repeatedly
  • Detailing concerns that are easy to underestimate
  • Material-specific lessons learned
  • Questions a reviewer should ask before approval

Why it matters

Better projects come from better shared judgment.

Structural engineering knowledge is often trapped in individual experience. This network is intended to make that experience more useful across projects, jurisdictions, and design teams—without turning every specialist into the stamping engineer.

The best review is not just about checking grout space, slab thickness, or a generic detail note. It is about understanding whether the structural system is coherent, efficient, buildable, and safe.
