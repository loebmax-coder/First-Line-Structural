Why review needs depth

Good review is not about catching typos. It is about asking the questions that protect the project.

Some projects are ordinary. Others carry details, materials, code pathways, sequencing, or architectural ambitions that deserve a more specialized look. A two-story house may not need a national specialist. A 12-story mass timber housing project should be reviewed by someone who understands the system, the failure modes, and the common construction pitfalls.

Review in practice

Focused comments where coordination, load path, and constructability matter.

Our review process looks for the project-specific conditions that may deserve a closer look: unclear transfer points, connection assumptions, diaphragm behavior, system continuity, and details that may create field questions if left unresolved.

Why expert overview matters

Expanding access to experience improves the entire field.

More than a standard engineering review, we bring additional perspective to identify areas that may benefit from closer attention and refinement.

Structural engineering improves when lessons are shared across projects. By drawing on a broad network of experienced engineers, we are able to identify patterns—where designs commonly struggle, where details tend to create field coordination questions, and where assumptions deserve a second look. This approach helps refine not just individual projects, but the consistency and quality of structural design across the industry.

However, this expanded access to expertise does not dilute responsibility. First Line Structural maintains full control and liability over the design review service. Expert input is used to sharpen our review, not replace it.
